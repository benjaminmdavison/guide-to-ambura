Bramblewood

Geography and Climate

Bramblewood is a Halfling village located in a large, thick forest in the Sea of Grass. It is about twenty miles west of the Free City of Porphyrium.

The approach road to Bramblewood is rough and unimproved. It winds a narrow path through the hills of the Sea of Grass. While mounts can navigate the road easily enough, it is notoriously slow going for carts - and the wild fauna of the Sea of Grass pose many challenges to those walking the road. This has limited commerce between the Kingdom of Breland and Bramblewood, but the Halflings seem content to be left alone among the grass.

A rather shabby logging road runs from Bramblewood towards the Western Sea. The Halflings will cut timber from nearby outcroppings and haul the logs back to Bramblewood for milling. Wildmen, hermits, fishers, and other solitary creatures sometimes wander the road, but that is a rare occurrence.

Bramblewood itself is walled off from the Sea of Grass by a series of cunning defenses. An outer palisade of spiked logs sits just in front of a deep trench that has been dug around the outside perimeter of the city. The Halflings have planted tough thornbriar hedges around the base of the palisade. The gates are monitored by guards and can be closed and barred in a matter of seconds through a cunning release mechanism. Beyond the palisade, Bramblewood's inner wall is comprised of cleverly woven roots and branches that define the outline of their forest. The city is not impenetrable, but it is defensible.

City Amenities

Bramblewood is not huge but it offers access to most any amenity needed. A sampling of shops:

  • Selm's Snacks: a local Halfling bakery selling bread, pastries, cakes, and more.
  • The Wildcat: the most popular local inn. Offers lodgings for halflings, dwarves, and small humans or elves. Has a stable, serves food and drink.
  • The Grass Stalker: Across the street from The Wildcat. Caters to larger (human- and elf-sized) visitors.
  • The Infirmary: A place of rest where one can find healers, salves, and potions.
  • Tally's Whacker: A cheekily-named shop selling forged goods. Run by Tally himself. Sells farm implements, basic weapons, horseshoes, etc.
  • Sawgrass Outfitters: Sells basic adventuring supplies, specializing in the Sea of Grass. Books and manages expeditions, caravans, etc.
  • Grass Market: merchants and vendors sell and trade all manner of goods here. You can find produce, meat, baked goods, finery, and pretty much anything else if you wander the market. Market Days are thrice weekly.
  • Wasp-Spittle Press: An industrious little printing press that produces pamphlets, menus, and full-scale bound books.

City Structure

Bramblewood itself sits around a wide-open green with a large, deep pond at the center. Footpaths through the green wind into the forest. While the cities of men and dwarves are rigidly engineered, Bramblewood has grown organically, and the Halfling village seems disorganized or even chaotic to outsiders: there is no central trading district, and little to no signage. Small gardens of flowers and vegetables are randomly planted in patches of filtered sun between trees.

The Halflings live in traditional Halfling burrows. Burrows vary widely: some are simple and fairly shallow, with only a few rooms; others are much deeper and more complex, like rodent warrens. Some, but not all, burrows are connected by underground tunnels. The Halflings of Bramblewood have also expanded upwards. Many burrows are connected to elaborate staircases leading to treehouses, and the Halflings commonly have tea and parties among the treetops, especially when the moon is full.

The Halflings run their village through a cooperative, elder-based government. The rulers of the village are the Counsel of Five. The main character on the Counsel is a very old Halfling referred to only as [[Gran]]. Alongside Gran sit four other elders: Old Carl, Bosher, Griselda, and Poppy.

Fun-loving and playful, yet industrious, the Halflings of Bramblewood are skilled makers. They have a reputation as excellent chefs, and they forge and smith exceptional cutlery and cookware. Bramblewood is home to a printing-press, a paper mill, and an assortment of small craftsmen and artisans. They are skilled woodworkers and carve ornate pipes, bookends, and furnishings. Most of their weapons are made by the [[Borovian Dwarves]] and scaled to Halfling proportions.

There are a few magic-users among the Halflings, mostly acting as healers and charmers. One of the only outsiders to reside in Bramblewood is an ancient gnome named [[Sigmin]], who resides in a treehouse and spends much time peering into a telescope and doing astrological calculations.

The city is guarded by a dedicated team of Halflings who act as constables and soldiers. While they are a non-violent and congenial people, the Halflings have long memories of horrible men and recent experience with dangerous beasts. Twice a year, they train in surface and subsurface warfare with a detachment of Borovian Dwarves, with whom they have a friendly relationship.
