Brelas Shield

Brelas’ Shield is the capital city of the Kingdom of Breland. The city is named for the ancient warrior who established the city after defeating a band of wild men in the area. The fabled knight Brelas carved his fortification - known as the Shield - out of a hillside, burrowing into the hill and surrounding it with a series of circular palisades that have since been fortified by stone. The fortification itself is now home to King Philip Wyrmbane the Fair.
The city has expanded outward in concentric circles from the hillside. The western district, near the River of Diamonds, is very crowded with businesses, as the wide, deep river offers a convenient means of transportation. The eastern district is more spaced out and expansive, and features small farms, manor houses, and residential block-houses for laborers. 
As the seat of the Kingdom, Brelas’ Shield is occupied by many prominent citizens. Wealthy nobles, knights, and merchants maintain urban estates alongside the outermost walls of the Shield itself. There is an embassy of Dwarves from Norovia, and a handful of mysterious Elves who come from the Western Isles but do not have any official diplomatic capacity. 
The southern district of Brelas’ Shield is rougher. River-workers and miners live here, and often get into loud and profanity-laden arguments about whose lives are more difficult and miserable. 
Citizens who can afford it usually travel to or from Brelas’ Shield on the river. For everyone else, there is an overland passage to the Screelands that is relatively well-traveled and safe. Merchant caravans often include a few wagons for travelers.

Castle District

- Seat of government; large, impressive fort on the hillside; garrison of soldiers. - Aqueduct-type structure running to river and private boathouse. - Just outside gates: diplomatic mission from Borovia; small, non-official delegation of Elfin merchants who keep to themselves.

Western District

- Docks - Warehouses, stables, livery-houses - Charter river boats - Fish market and general market - Mill - The Millwright: fine dining - The Dodgy Dockman: cheap inn, clean enough, not great.

Eastern District

- Farms, manors, block-houses; a few mines. - The Calloused Hand: farm and mining supplies, smithy - Berry Farm Manor: upscale inn - The Work House: place for day laborers

Southern District

- River-workers and miners, rough bars - The Rich Seam: low-end bar for miners - The Second Hand: used goods store, maybe fence -
